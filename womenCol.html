Women of Color

Women in Media

Overview

Women are often depicted differently in media based on their race. This is evident evident in stereotypes and characterization.

Racial Stereotyping

Negative or exaggerated stereotypes often influence the portrayal of non-caucasian characters. These portrayals in media reinforce pre-existing prejudices and beliefs based on race. This can be very harmful as people may become conditioned, subconsciously, to treat people based on these. Some common stereotypes are listed below:

The Mellow

Asians are depicted as being submissive and subservient, particularly Asian women.

The Ninja

Quiet Asian characters that are mysterious and often goes with martial arts.

The Sassy/The Agressive

Particularly black women are depicted as being aggressive or rude, particularly to men. Combines with the 'Angry Black Woman' stereotype.

The Jezebel

Overly sexualized and depicted as being exploitative for money.

The Sexpots

Applies to the portrayal of many POC women in media, especially towards latina or black women. Latina and Hispanic women are depicted as being provocative, often drinking heavily.

Hypersexualization

Women are often sexualized to different extents based on their race. For instance, it is shown more in depictions of black or latina women while asians can be fetishized.

Exoticism

Women of Color are often precieved as being 'exotic' or foreign. This ties in to how they are sexualized as being more 'wild' or 'free-spirited'.
